4 - Taking off

You are part of a NASA team in charge of designing software to help astronauts in their missions. You are assigned a task to write a program to help astronauts to configure their spaceships when taking off exoplanets. For that, they must beat the gravitational attraction of the planet. The attraction is a force which intensity is (roughly) given by the following formula :

$$ F = G \frac{M}{d^2} $$

Where :

  • \(G\) is the gravitational constant : \(G \approx 6.67.10^{-11} m^3.kg.s^{-2}\)
  • \(M\) is the mass of the planet in \(kg\)
  • \(d\) is the distance to the centre of the planet in \(m\)
Write a program to automatically detect if the spaceship configuration is powerful enough to take off.

For that :

  1. Ask the user the mass of the planet \(M\)
  2. Ask the user their curent distance from the centre \(d\)
  3. Ask the user the propulsion force of their spaceship \(F_p\)
  4. Compute \(F\) from \(M\) and \(d\).
  5. Compare \(F\) with the propulsion intensity and print on the screen if the astronaut will be able to take off or not

Test your program with the following test cases :

  • Earth case 1 : Take off is possible
    • \(M = 5.972.10^{24} kg\)
    • \(d = 6.371.10^6 m \)
    • \(F_p = 10.0 N\)
  • Earth case 2 : Take off is not possible
    • \(M = 5.972.10^{24} kg\)
    • \(d = 6.371.10^6 m \)
    • \(F_p = 4.0 N\)
  • Moon case : Take off is possible
    • \(M = 7.347.10^{22} kg\)
    • \(d = 1.737.10^6 m \)
    • \(F_p = 5.0 N\)
  • Ganymede case : Take off is not possible
    • \(M = 1.481.10^{23} kg\)
    • \(d = 5.262.10^6 m \)
    • \(F_p = 1.0 N\)
