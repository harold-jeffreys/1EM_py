Arrays and Data Types

Make sure you have read unit 4 before attempting the following exercise.

The inverse of a 2x2 matrix is given by $$ \left(\begin{array}{cc} a & b \\ c & d \end{array}\right)^{-1}= \frac{1}{ad-bc}\left(\begin{array}{cc} d & -b \\ -c & a \end{array}\right) $$
Write a program to calculate the inverse of a 2x2 matrix, using arrays to represent the matrices. You can pick the elements of the matrix to be anything you want, but check the result by using MATMUL to multiply the inverse by the original matrix to obtain the identity.
The MATMUL page contains a useful way of initialising arrays, which you can use.
Now, modify your program to create a data type holding two 2x2 arrays. Declare a variable of this type, and initialise it with your matrix from above, and its inverse. Print the contents of your new type-variable with WRITE(6,*) and examine the output.

When you are happy with your program, it's time to go on to Unit 5.
