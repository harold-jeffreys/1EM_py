1 - Projectile

Write a program to calculate the trajectory of an object. The object is located at the origin at t=0. The initial $x$- and $y$-components of the velocity should be entered at the keyboard. You can set the acceleration in the $x$-direction to be zero, and in the $y$-direction to be $-9.8\textrm{ms}^{-2}$ within the body of your program. Your program should calculate the x and y positions of the object every $0.01$s for $1000$ time steps.
The object will move according to Newton's laws, but we will (later) have a non-constant acceleration so the "suvat" equations don't apply. However, what we can do is use these equations and apply them many times over successive tiny time intervals (as you saw in exercise 5) over which we can assume the acceleration is constant. The length of the small time interval can be made larger or smaller depending on how accurately we want the answer.
Newton's equations of motion are

x_new = x + ux*tint + 0.5*ax*(tint**2)
ux_new = ux + ax*tint

where x_new is the new value of x at a time tint=0.01 seconds later. ux, uy, ax and ay are the velocity components and acceleration components respectively. You should use these equations (and equivalent ones in the $y$ direction) at each time step to update the coordinates and velocity components.

Your program should ensure that only positive values of ux and uy are entered. You will need to use one or more if ... statements here. Arrange for your program to stop (i.e. break out of the loop) as soon as y is less than zero (we don't want our object burrowing through the ground!).

Plan your program first on paper either as a flow chart or in pseudocode. Think about the order in which you need to do things, and the quantities you need to calculate.

Program extensions

Now let's assess the effect of air resistance. Have the $x$ component of acceleration, ax, dependent on ux. For exampleax = -0.05*ux. This is an easy extension.

Try to get your object to bounce! In other words, as soon as y is less than zero, change uy to be positive. Halve the values of ux and uy after a bounce. This is not so easy to implement.
